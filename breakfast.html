STEAMED UPMA

An easy and quick breakfast recipe prepared by steaming the spiced semolina based balls.
It is an easy and quick recipe, providing all the necessary nutrients for the morning diet.
It is served and eaten as it is without any side dish, but tastes exceptionally great with a choice of chutney recipes.

POHA

Poha, also known as flattened rice or beaten rice,
is a popular Indian dish, especially for breakfast, made from flattened rice flakes.
It's known for being a quick, easy, and healthy meal.
Poha is typically cooked with onions, mustard seeds, curry leaves, and spices, and often includes peanuts, potatoes, and other vegetables.

OATS AND BEETROOT DOSA

Oats and Beetroot Dosa is a healthy, instant, and
colorful South Indian crepe that combines the fiber of oats,
the nutrients of beetroot, and often rice flour or semolina for a delicious,
no-fermentation dish perfect for breakfast or a quick meal.

ONION RICE

Onion dosa is a popular variation of the South Indian rice-and-lentil crepe (dosa),
made by adding finely chopped onions to the batter or topping them on the hot griddle before cooking.

PULIYOGARE

Puliyogare or simply Tamarind rice, is a common and traditional rice preparation in the South Indian states of Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Kerala, Tamil Nadu and Karnataka.

NEER DOSA

Neer Dosa is a popular, delicate South Indian (specifically Mangalorean) rice-based crepe, translating to "water dosa" due to its very thin, watery batter.
It's made by grinding soaked rice into a smooth paste, mixing it with water and salt to achieve a runny consistency, and then pouring it onto a hot pan to cook quickly on one side, creating a lacy, soft, and translucent texture.

TOMATO SEMIYA

This is an excellent, simple, and tasty South Indian breakfast recipe prepared with non-sticky vermicelli noodles and ripe tomato masala.
It is an extension of the popular semiya upma recipe, which is made with just onion and basic spice masala.
